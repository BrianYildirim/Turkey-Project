Culture


Foods.

A know Turkish dessert is the Turkish delight. The Turkish delight is most commonly served with black tea to guests as a sweet to calm the bitterness of the tea. The baklava is also a great choice for dessert, containing pistachios, sweet syrups, and pastry dough.



Customs.

  • Greetings Among Men: When two men meet for the first time, they shake hands and make direct eye contact. Greetings with friends or family is a bit less serious, such as being able to pat their backs.

  • Greetings Among Women: For initial encounters, a light handshake is apropriate. Meeting friends, women can kiss each other's cheek while giving them a light hug.

  • Men Greeting Women: If you are the male, I suggest waiting for your cue to shake hands. When she offers you her hand, you shake it. If their cheek is offered, you may kiss it.

Language.

Turkey has a main language that the majority speaks, and it would be Turkish, obviously. Some people in other parts of Turkey speak other languages, such as Arabic or Kurdish, but if you don't know Turkish in Turkey, there is no hope for you. Some phrases are "merhaba", meaning "hello", and "teşekkürler", meaning "thank you."



Holidays.

Holidays celebrated in Turkey that aren't celebrated much in the U.S.A is National Children's day, celebrating the birth of all children in Turkey. Children recieve money, treats, toys, and any wishes they want is theirs. It is a gift from the parents to let the children know the parents would do anything for them.
